# 📊 Margin Performance & Pricing Analytics on Qlik & Power BI
**Analyzing gross margin performance and pricing insights using Qlik**

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyses the gross margin performance of shipped orders across multiple business dimensions including time, customers, products, warehouses, and product categories using Qlik and Power BI as two main business intelligence tools, demonstrating transferable BI tool ultilisation and problem solving skills.  

The goal is to evaluate overall profitability, detect margin risks, and identify opportunities for pricing optimisation and product mix improvements. The analysis covers **98.5K orders and $42.5M in revenue**, providing insights into margin trends and profitability drivers.

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
- **Qlik Sense** – dashboard development and interactive analytics
- **Qlik Script** – data extraction and transformation
- **Power BI** - Smaller extraction analysis
- **DAX** - Data transformation on Power BI
- **Data Modelling** – revenue, cost and margin calculations
- **Business Analytics** – profitability segmentation and performance monitoring

## 📊 Key Insights
- **Overall Profitability**: The business generated **$42.53M revenue** with an average gross margin of **36.11%**, indicating generally healthy profitability levels.
- **Margin Trend Risk**: Gross margin declined significantly from **37.00% in 2025 to 21.98% in 2026**, suggesting potential pricing pressure or rising procurement costs.
- **Profit Concentration in Key SKUs**: A small number of products contributed disproportionately to total profit, highlighting opportunities to optimise product mix.
- **Margin Leakage Detection**: Several items generated negative margins, indicating potential pricing inconsistencies or cost inefficiencies.
- **Customer Profitability Variation**: Some customers delivered margins above **50%**, while others generated significant losses, highlighting opportunities for pricing review.
- **Category Performance**: Outdoor Living delivered the strongest margin performance (**42.06%**), while Home Living showed lower profitability (**20.96%**).
